In our initial user session, our participant highlighted that they found it difficult to assess some of the entries text. They said that it often referred to ‘the land’ but as the map view focuses on a single extent, they found it difficult to understand the title as a whole and how the reference extent relates to the whole plot of land.

When asked what they needed, they suggested that the property description would help as it describes the title. It is something they are used to seeing from case working experience on applications.

The property description is usually entry A1 in the register and relates to a red boundary title extent.

Currently, we are not including title extents in our data for proof of concept but we are including all entries including A1s.

We don’t know that our users will have the same experience and expectations of seeing a property description, but we want to understand if this would help them to complete the annotation more accurately.

We added an example to the prototype of an annotation page with a property description.

We noticed that the property description would be repeated when the first entry A1 is being annotated. In our sample of synthetic data only 2 of 11 titles have included this. It could be more common in a larger sample unless we decide not to annotate these entries.

We need to understand if:

  • the entry A1 always provides an accurate property description
  • the property description helps to make sense of individual entries and reference extents

We may also explore other ways to give users more contextual information about a title, such as showing the title boundary with the selected extent.
